# Fufluns

Etruscan god of growth
In
Etruscan religion
,
Fufluns
(
Etruscan
:
𐌚𐌖𐌚𐌋𐌖𐌍𐌔
) or
Puphluns
(
Etruscan
:
𐌐𐌖𐌘𐌋𐌖𐌍𐌔
) was a god of plant life, happiness, wine, health, and growth in all things. He is mentioned twice among the gods listed in the inscriptions of the
Liver of Piacenza
, being listed among the 16
gods that rule the Etruscan astrological houses. He is the 9th of those 16
gods.
[
1
]
He is the son of
Semla
and the god
Tinia
. He was worshipped at
Populonia
(Etruscan
Fufluna
or
Pupluna
) and is the namesake of that town.
[
2
]
His Greek equivalent is
Dionysos
(Latin Dionysus), whereas his Roman equivalent is
Liber
. For this reason he was also called Fufluns Pachies or Pacha.
